Welcome to the Garden

I have had some sort of web presence/blog since about 1998. I made my own websites at first, journaled on various forums, had a LiveJournal, many Wordpress blogs, and have used various social medias as a type of diary. None of these ways of capturing my thoughts for public consumption quite fit the bill of how I wanted to express myself online. While I really enjoyed the community feel of forums and social media, there was always a pressure to respond and read everyone else before posting my own words. Having a blog felt too formal. Like everything had to be just right before I could put it out to the world. Polished, with the right photo. Tags, categories, date created. Then it was "out there," never to be touched or updated again. I'm a bit obsessive when it comes to these things and once the thing was out there with a published date, I would feel like I couldn't go back to it and change it. Where would it fit? With the old date or a new one?

I have been using Obsidian for note taking for quite awhile now, and enjoy the flexibility of undated, living, evolving notes and writings. When books had to written out by hand by monks, when pamphlets had to be printed at the printing press, when the only way to look up a fact was in the encyclopedia, perhaps it made sense to think of things as needing to be polished and finished before the expense was put forward to make a hard copy of a story, fact, or essay. But, we live in the age of digital ink. While I want my news to be trustworthy and researched fully (which it rarely is), for the lay person who is keeping a blog or finding a way to publish their ideas, there is no reason for a polished final product.

I have become jaded on the idea of keeping a formal "blog," yet wanted to still have an informal online presence. As I went down the rabbit hole of options, I came across the idea of a "digital garden." A place to learn in public, explore half formed ideas, and link one thought to another through building a "second brain."

I have been using Obsidian for note taking for about a year and it was the idea of linking notes and keeping "evergreen" notes that lead me to start a garden of my own.

A reverse chronologically organized space with set dates implying that these things were finished, never to be returned to and updated, wasn't working for my curiosity driven, ever changing brain. Feeling the need to have every thought fleshed out and finished before sharing made me just not write at all. Feeling like the space needed to be polished in a perfect SEO driven format with perfectly aesthetic and glossy photos wasn't my style either. I enjoyed playing. Playing with graphics and fonts and layouts, but that took away from the actual writing. I also didn't want to be a "content creator." I want to be a real person putting real thoughts on a page to connect with other real people out there.

This is the word playground that my blog could never be. Here I plan to play with many different ideas (another thing that is discouraged in the world of blogging where every blogger must blog about one thing and only that one thing), publish things I'm curious about and not worry if everything is polished.

There are many other digital gardens or second brains out there to explore and many tools for building them.

I have built this garden by publishing my Obsidian notes with Obsidian Publish. There are many ways to publish an Obsidian vault for free, but at this time I lack the technical skills to undertake them and just want to get stared tending my garden.

Welcome to my garden and I hope you find a few rabbit holes to fall into while you're here.

Right now the garden contains a few different stages of growth:

00 Kelli Anna

These are just random notes, lists, and things about me. A place to get to know me, a song of myself.

00 Seeds

These are just ideas for future use. Ideas for creative pursuits, writings, this garden, the future

02 seedlings

These are unfinished beginnings of writings and other pursuits. Half baked ideas, things to return to when the time is right.

03 flowers

Here is where the more polished, "finished" work resides. Maybe these will still be edited in the future, but for the most part they have reached the last stage of growth and are at their most beautiful.

04 rabbit holes

Topics I have fallen into and lost myself in for awhile. Nothing complete, nothing publishable, just learning for fun and mostly for myself. Learning should be a life long endeavor of rabbit holes and curiosities.
